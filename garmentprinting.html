Garment and UV Printing



This week's task was to design and print our own T-shirt, using the Brother GT-361 printer.


This is the original picture of my dog that I chose to put on my T-shirt.




Then, using Adobe Photoshop, I touched up the photo a bit, and cropped it as I want it to show on my T-shirt. This is the picture after the process.




Then, I opened up the file in a program called "PDIP 1.2" and adjusted the picture to a place where I wanted it to be printed on the shirt.




This is my final product of my T-shirt Printing!










UV printing



Using the UV printer, I printed a picture on my phone case.
For my design, I cut out part of the black background of a picture using coreldraw.
Then, I saved it as .EPS file.
So, here is my design




Because my phone is iphone 5c that nobody uses, I had to trace my phonecase on a cardboard and cut out the line.



I set up the UV printer and measured the height and area of the phone case using automatic measure.
Then, using Roland Versawork, I set up the position and the size of the image and printed!

Here is my result!
I had a sticker on my case, so when I removed it, there was sticky stuff left on the case, so on the right side of it, it didn't print out correctly.
