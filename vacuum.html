Making a Personalized Skateboard Deck




Lamination



First, using the Tirebond III glue, I laminated my skateboard.


7 layers of pre-cut wooden boards were glued together in the following order.




Vacuum Forming



Before the glue dried up, I used vacuum forming to form the skateboard.
After placing the board on the mold, I taped them together to make sure the skateboard stay in place
Then I placed it in a vacuum forming bag
Before closing the bag, I placed a mesh on the board in order to make sure that the air in every corner comes out evenly.
I closed up the bag, and pumped the air out
This process took me a while because I was pumping out the air without realizing the bag wasn't sealed completely.








I also found that my name on the top part of the case wasn't clear. If I had made the resolution to be higher, that might have helped.






3D printing the stamp case




I decided to create a stamp box for the stamp I made. I designed a box, using fusion360.
I wanted the height of the lower part to be shorter than the height of the stamp to make it easier to take the stamp out.
I also created grooves on the sides of the top part to make it easier to open.
Then, I added the design of the stamp on top of the case to show which stamp is in it.


This is what the case looks like after printing.






I'm so happy that it came out exactly as I wanted to be.







3D scanning and printing myself






Using Knockout Concepts Mobile scanner, I scanned myself and made a 3D model of myself.

I brought it down to a miniature size (1:55) and saved as a .makerbot file

This is my final product! It looks like a soldier from toy story and I love it!
